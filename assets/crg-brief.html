Consumer Relationship Guide — Plain English

Alberta Real Estate Council (RECA) requires every agent to provide this information before working with you. Here's what it actually means.

Quick answer: This is not a contract. It's an explanation of your rights and how the agent–client relationship works. Nothing you sign here locks you in.

What is the CRG?

The Consumer Relationship Guide is a mandatory disclosure document required by RECA (Alberta's real estate regulator). Every licensed agent must give it to you before providing any real estate services. It explains two different ways you can work with a REALTOR®:

Relationship What it means Best for
Client Recommended The agent represents you exclusively. They have full fiduciary duties: loyalty, confidentiality, full disclosure, and the obligation to negotiate in your best interest. Anyone serious about buying a home in Calgary
Customer The agent assists you (provides information, shows homes) but does not represent you. They have no duty to put your interests first. Very rare; usually applies when no agency agreement exists

What does "representing you" actually look like?

  • I research comparable sales to help you make a competitive — not over-priced — offer
  • I advise you on offer conditions, inspection results, and negotiation tactics
  • I keep your budget, motivation, and timeline confidential from the seller
  • I flag anything that could be a problem with the property or the deal
  • I work to get you the best possible price and terms

Does it cost me anything extra to have an agent represent me?

No. In Alberta, the seller pays the commission — both for their own agent and for your agent. As a buyer working with Rosh, you pay nothing directly for agent representation. (This may change with future industry rules, but will always be disclosed to you upfront.)

Can I work with multiple agents at the same time?

Yes — unless you sign an exclusive Buyer Representation Agreement (BRA). If you sign a BRA, you agree to work exclusively with that agent for the specified period. Rosh will never pressure you into signing a BRA before you're ready.

What happens if Rosh represents both me and the seller?

This is called transaction brokerage. If Rosh is also the listing agent for a home you want to buy, Alberta law requires both parties to consent in writing. In that situation, Rosh becomes a neutral facilitator rather than an advocate for either party. You'll always be informed before this happens.

Rosh's recommendation: Let's work as agent and client from the start. It costs you nothing, protects your interests, and means I'm fully in your corner at every step — from search to keys in hand.
